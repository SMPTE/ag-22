Introduction

Review of an engineering document by the public, beyond the members of the SMPTE Standards Community, can help determine if it meets industry needs and satisfies interoperability requirements. Such a review ideally takes place before the engineering document reaches Final Committee Draft, after which making substantial changes becomes increasingly complex.

This Administrative Guideline specifies the process for Public Committee Drafts (Public CD), which are Committee Drafts made available to the public for review.

A Public CD was referred to as a Technical Specification between 2018 and 2019.

Public CD Development

General (Informative)

The development of a Public CD is conducted according to the SMPTE , including, but not limited to:

  • the decision to make a Committee Draft available to the public rests with the Standards Vice President (, Section 9.3.1);
  • contributions to a Public CD are subject to intellectual property and confidentiality policy specified in , Section 9;
  • decisions are based on consensus, as defined in , Section 4.1;
  • the Project Group can return the Public CD to the Technology Committee Chair for FCD ballot at any time (, Section 6.5).

Project Proposal

A Project Proposal should indicate whether a deliverable is intended to become a Public CD.

Creation

Any Committee Draft may be made available as a Public CD if it meets the following criteria:

  • the Committee Draft has not been previously made available as a Public CD and subsequently terminated, as specified in .
  • by Consensus and usually following a recommendation of a Project Group, the TC has recommended to the Standards Vice President that the Committee Draft be made available as a Public CD.

The TC recommendation should include a brief text appropriate for a public announcement of the Public CD.

When seeking the TC recommendation, the TC chair should use the notice specified in .

Availability

The Public CD shall be made available to the public in HTML format as defined by in a public GitHub repo as defined by , approved by the Director of Standards Development, and shall be accompanied by the notices specified in .

Comment Period

A Project Group should, but is under no obligation to, address comments received from the public on a Public CD.

The review period of a Public CD starts upon availability, as defined in , and ends when either:

  • the document begins its FCD ballot (, Section 6.6), at which point the notice specified in shall be displayed prominently alongside the Public CD; or
  • the Public CD is terminated.

Revision

A Public CD may be revised at any time.

To revise a Public CD, the Committee Draft shall be revised.

Revising a Committee Draft returns the document to Working Draft status and the process at , Section 6.5 is followed to elevate the revised Working Draft to Committee Draft.

The revised Committee Draft may follow the process of to be made available as a Public CD.

Termination

A Public CD shall be terminated if any one of the following criteria is satisfied:

  • the engineering document is published (see , Section 6.11)
  • the Committee Draft of the document is revised
  • more than 1095 days have elapsed since the document was initially made available as a Public CD, unless the Standards Vice President elects to extend the period upon recommendation of the TC
  • the Project authorizing the engineering document is terminated

Upon termination of a Public CD, its public access to shall be immediately terminated.
